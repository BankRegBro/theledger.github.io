What Is Claude Mythos?

Claude Mythos is Anthropic's most powerful AI model ever built — a tier above its public Opus line, first revealed not through a press release but through an accidental data exposure on March 26, 2026. Its internal codename was Capybara.

What distinguishes Mythos from all prior models is not merely raw capability — it is Anthropic's own publicly stated fear of what the model can do in the wrong hands. Mythos can autonomously identify and exploit software vulnerabilities, including flaws that had evaded expert human review for decades, across every major operating system and browser it was tested against. Anthropic found it "exceeds all but the most skilled humans" in detecting security weaknesses.

Project Glasswing — The Defensive Response

Rather than release Mythos publicly, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing on April 7, 2026 — named for the glasswing butterfly whose transparent wings hide in plain sight. It provides controlled access exclusively for defensive cybersecurity work, backed by a $100M usage credit pool and $4M in open-source security donations.

Six structural concerns driving regulatory urgency across global financial supervisors:

⚡
Unprecedented Vulnerability Discovery Speed
Mythos can surface decades-old bugs — including a 27-year-old flaw in OpenBSD and critical issues across every major OS and browser — at a pace that outstrips entire human security teams. Elite researchers typically require weeks or months for what Mythos achieves in hours.
🔗
Autonomous Exploit Chaining
The model doesn't just find bugs — it can construct working exploits without human guidance. Palo Alto Networks warns this shifts the paradigm from AI-assisted to AI-driven attacks, compressing attack cycle times from days or weeks to minutes.
🏦
Financial Sector Concentration Risk
Unlike dispersed critical infrastructure, banking is highly consolidated. Bank of England Governor Andrew Bailey noted the model "makes it easier to detect existing vulnerabilities in core IT systems" that cybercriminals could exploit — at scale, across interconnected institutions.
🔓
Access Control Integrity
Mythos' restricted access has already been compromised: individuals linked to an Anthropic contractor guessed the model's endpoint using leaked naming conventions. Anthropic itself had two disclosure incidents in the weeks before launch, raising questions about containment.
📋
Regulatory Lag
The EU AI Act's next enforcement phase doesn't take effect until August 2026. Most banks are operating on patching cycles designed for a 2019 threat surface. The window between vulnerability disclosure and weaponization has compressed from 19 days (2023) to under 72 hours today.
⚖️
Dual-Mandate Tension
Regulators face a structural dilemma: encourage AI adoption for efficiency gains while preventing the same AI from becoming an attack vector. The White House simultaneously encouraged defensive deployment and raised national security objections to broader access.
Regulator's Core Dilemma

As one expert framed it: "Frontier AI systems don't create a new category of risk — they collapse the timelines of every existing one." Banks are defending 2026 attacks with 2019 standard operating procedures. Regulators must simultaneously push institutions toward AI-driven efficiency gains while preventing the same AI from becoming the attack vector against the infrastructure it is meant to modernize.

The Skeptic's View

Not everyone has accepted the alarm at face value. Security expert Bruce Schneier called the launch "very much a PR play by Anthropic — and it worked," noting reporters were "breathlessly repeating Anthropic's talking points without engaging critically."

Proofpoint CSO Ryan Kalember offered a simpler prediction: "I think this gets solved on its own, because the other models will catch up." He added: "We're not seeing EternalBlue or world-melting vulnerabilities fall out of it." His view — that banks are unusually well-resourced and the financial sector warrants attention but not panic — represents a meaningful counterweight to the regulatory emergency framing.
