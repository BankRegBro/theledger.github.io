    Three through-lines

    Federalism & chartering authority Administrative process Tailored, dual-banking substance

    Federalism & chartering authority — jealous defense of state chartering and licensing against OCC preemption and CFPB sandbox gambits. Administrative process — federal agencies repeatedly issuing major rules without rigorous data, APA-compliant process, or interagency alignment. Tailored, dual-banking substance — support for tailoring, BSA/AML modernization, NMLS networked supervision, and a state-led path for nonbanks (fintech, money transmission, stablecoins).

    CEO eras (color band beneath the timeline)

    Letters are color-coded by the CSBS President & CEO during whose tenure they were sent — not a claim about individual signatories. Ryan (through May 14, 2022); Cooper (May 15, 2022 – Dec. 3, 2023, following John W. Ryan's death in May 2022); Milhorn (Dec. 4, 2023 – present). The tone of letters from late 2023 forward is measurably more critical of federal banking-agency proposals than letters before that date, even where the underlying substantive position is unchanged.

    Methodology & caveats

    This compendium covers letters dated Jan. 13, 2017 onward. CSBS's own comment-letter index lists 144 results within Dec. 31, 2016 – May 15, 2026; one of those is a link to the pre-2017 archive, and a second is reflected here by inference rather than retrieved PDF — leaving 142 substantive entries in this set. Letters with curated PDF/landing-page URLs link directly; others link to a pre-filled search of csbs.org/comment-letters. Quotations are drawn from the letter PDF where retrieved, otherwise from CSBS-hosted HTML landing pages or press releases. Vehicle classifications (Rulemaking / ANPR / RFI / Bill or Draft / Nomination / Joint letter / Other) are inferred from the recipient and addressee fields and may be coarse at the margins.
